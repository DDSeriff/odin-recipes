Tiramisu

Is there a name for a dessert that tempts you in ways that you never thought possible? A dessert that can lead the strongest of diets astray, and bend the strongest of wills to its sweet desires? There is. And its name is tiramisu.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups (355 ml) of heavy cream
  • 8 ounces (227 grams) of mascarpone cheese, brought to room temperature
  • 1/3 cup (113 grams) of granulated white sugar
  • 1 teaspoon (5 ml) vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups (355 ml) of cold espresso
  • 3 tablespoons (45 ml) of coffee flavored liqueur
  • 1 package of lady fingers
  • Cocoa powder for dusting the top of your tiramisu

    • Steps

    1. Add your whipping cream to a large mixing bowl and beat on medium speed with an electric mixer. A stand mixer is ideal. Slowly add sugar and vanilla and continue beating until stiff peaks are formed in the cream. Add the mascarpone cheese and continue to mix until combined.
    2. Add coffee and liqueur to a shallow bowl. Quickly dip the lady fingers in the mixture, careful not so soak them. Lay your dipped lady fingers in an 8x8 (or similar) pan.
    3. Smooth half of the mixture from step one over the top of your dipped fingers. Add another layer of dipped fingers, and again add more of the mixture, smoothing it over the top.
    4. Dust cocoa powder over the top and refrigerate for 3-4 hours or up to overnight before serving.
